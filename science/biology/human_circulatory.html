1. Blood — Composition & Functions

Blood is a fluid connective tissue that circulates through the heart, arteries, capillaries, and veins. Total volume: ~5-6 L (adult, 7-8% of body weight). pH: 7.35-7.45 (slightly alkaline).

  • Plasma (55%) — liquid matrix (92% water + 7% proteins + 1% nutrients/hormones/wastes)
  • Formed elements (45%) — RBCs (~99% of cells), WBCs (~1%), Platelets
Plasma Proteins
  • Albumin — 60% of plasma proteins; maintains osmotic pressure, transports fatty acids
  • Globulins — 35%; alpha, beta (transport), gamma (antibodies = immunoglobulins)
  • Fibrinogen — 4%; blood clotting (→ fibrin)
  • Serum = plasma − clotting factors (fibrinogen removed)

2. Red Blood Cells (Erythrocytes)

  • Count: 4.5-5.5 million/mm³ (♂), 4-5 million/mm³ (♀)
  • Biconcave disc — increases surface area for gas exchange
  • No nucleus (in mammals) — more space for Hb; lifespan: 120 days
  • Produced in Red Bone Marrow (ribs, sternum, vertebrae); destroyed in Spleen & Liver
  • Hemoglobin (Hb) — 12-16 g/dL (♂), 11-14 g/dL (♀); each Hb carries 4 O₂ molecules
  • Erythropoietin (EPO) — hormone from kidney that stimulates RBC production
  • Anemia — low Hb/RBC count → fatigue, pallor
  • Polycythemia — excess RBCs → increased blood viscosity

3. White Blood Cells & Platelets

WBCs (Leukocytes) — 4000-11000/mm³. They are soldiers of the body — fight infections.

Type%Function
Neutrophils60-65%Phagocytosis of bacteria (first responders)
Lymphocytes20-25%B-cells (antibodies), T-cells (cellular immunity)
Monocytes6-8%Become macrophages in tissues; phagocytosis
Eosinophils2-4%Fight parasites; allergic reactions
Basophils0.5-1%Release histamine (inflammation, allergic response)
Platelets (Thrombocytes)
  • Count: 1.5-4 lakh/mm³; fragments of megakaryocytes (from bone marrow)
  • Lifespan: 5-9 days; function: Blood clotting (hemostasis)
  • Thrombocytopenia — low platelets → bleeding risk
  • Clotting process: Platelet plug → Clotting cascade → Fibrin mesh → Thrombus
  • Hemophilia — genetic deficiency of clotting factor VIII → prolonged bleeding
WBC Mnemonic: "Never Let Monkeys Eat Bananas"
Neutrophils → Lymphocytes → Monocytes → Eosinophils → Basophils (highest to lowest count)

4. Heart — Structure

  • Size: ~fist-sized; weight: 250-350 g; located in mediastinum (between lungs, behind sternum)
  • Walls: Pericardium (outer sac) → Myocardium (muscle — thickest) → Endocardium (inner lining)
Chambers
  • 4 chambers: Right Atrium (RA) — receives deoxygenated blood from body via Superior & Inferior Vena Cava
  • Right Ventricle (RV) — pumps deoxygenated blood to lungs via Pulmonary Artery
  • Left Atrium (LA) — receives oxygenated blood from lungs via Pulmonary Veins (4)
  • Left Ventricle (LV) — pumps oxygenated blood to body via Aorta (thickest wall — pumps against highest pressure)
Valves
  • Atrioventricular valves: Tricuspid (RA→RV), Bicuspid/Mitral (LA→LV)
  • Semilunar valves: Pulmonary (RV→pulmonary artery), Aortic (LV→aorta)
  • Valves prevent backflow of blood. "Lub-Dub" = closing of AV valves (lub) + semilunar valves (dub)

5. Cardiac Cycle & Output

  • One complete heartbeat = Systole (contraction) + Diastole (relaxation)
  • Duration: ~0.8 sec (at 72 bpm): 0.3 systole + 0.5 diastole
  • Cardiac Output (CO) = Heart Rate × Stroke Volume = 72 × 70 mL = ~5 L/min
  • Heart sounds: S1 (Lub) = AV valves close; S2 (Dub) = Semilunar valves close
  • SA Node (Sinoatrial — "Natural Pacemaker") → initiates impulse at 72/min
  • AV Node (Atrioventricular) → delays impulse (0.1 sec) → Bundle of His → Purkinje fibers
  • ECG (Electrocardiogram): P wave (atrial depolarization), QRS complex (ventricular depolarization), T wave (ventricular repolarization)
NCERT Connect
The heart beats approx 100,000 times per day, pumping ~7,500 L of blood through ~100,000 km of blood vessels!

Blood Pressure
  • Systolic BP (ventricular contraction): 120 mmHg (normal)
  • Diastolic BP (ventricular relaxation): 80 mmHg (normal)
  • Measured as 120/80 mmHg; Hypertension = > 140/90
  • Pulse = expansion & recoil of artery wall with each heartbeat (palpable at wrist, neck)

7. Types of Circulation

  • Pulmonary Circulation — Heart → Lungs → Heart (deoxy blood to lungs, oxy blood returns)
  • Systemic Circulation — Heart → Body → Heart (oxy blood to tissues, deoxy blood returns)
  • Coronary Circulation — heart muscle itself (coronary arteries supply heart — blockage → heart attack)
  • Hepatic Portal System — Vein from intestine → Liver (nutrients processed before going to heart)
  • Double circulation = Pulmonary + Systemic (advantage: oxygenated & deoxygenated blood kept separate)

8. Lymphatic System

  • Lymph — fluid from tissues that drains into lymph vessels; contains lymphocytes
  • Lymph nodes — filter lymph, produce antibodies
  • Spleen — largest lymphatic organ; filters blood, stores RBCs, immune response
  • Thymus — behind sternum; T-cell maturation (shrinks with age)
  • Tonsils — protect throat from pathogens
  • Functions: Drains excess tissue fluid, absorbs fats (lacteals in intestine), immune defense

10. Practice MCQs

1. Normal blood pH is:
A) 6.8-7.0
B) 7.0-7.2
C) 7.35-7.45
D) 7.5-7.8
Answer: C) 7.35-7.45 (slightly alkaline)
2. Which blood cells are most numerous?
A) WBCs
B) RBCs
C) Platelets
D) All equal
Answer: B) RBCs (4.5-5.5 million/mm³)
3. The natural pacemaker of the heart is:
A) AV Node
B) SA Node
C) Purkinje fibers
D) Bundle of His
Answer: B) SA Node (Sinoatrial node) — generates 72 impulses/min
4. Which blood vessel carries oxygenated blood from lungs to heart?
A) Pulmonary artery
B) Pulmonary vein
C) Aorta
D) Vena cava
Answer: B) Pulmonary vein (only vein carrying oxygenated blood)
5. WBC count in normal adult is:
A) 1000-2000/mm³
B) 4000-11000/mm³
C) 1-2 lakh/mm³
D) 4-5 million/mm³
Answer: B) 4000-11000/mm³
6. Blood clotting requires:
A) Vitamin A
B) Vitamin C
C) Vitamin K
D) Vitamin E
Answer: C) Vitamin K — for prothrombin synthesis in liver
7. Which chamber of heart has the thickest wall?
A) Right atrium
B) Right ventricle
C) Left atrium
D) Left ventricle
Answer: D) Left ventricle — pumps blood to entire body via aorta
8. Cardiac output is calculated as:
A) HR × BP
B) HR × SV
C) SV × BP
D) HR × PR
Answer: B) Heart Rate × Stroke Volume = ~5 L/min
