1. Introduction — Excretion

Excretion is the removal of metabolic waste products from the body. The excretory system includes kidneys, ureters, urinary bladder, urethra, skin, lungs, and liver.

  • Major excretory products: Urea (from protein metabolism), CO₂ (from respiration), Bile pigments (from Hb breakdown), Water & salts, Uric acid (from nucleic acids)
  • Excretion ≠ Egestion: Egestion = removal of undigested food (feces), not metabolic waste

2. Kidney — Structure

  • Paired, bean-shaped organs located on either side of spine (retroperitoneal, between T12-L3)
  • Size: ~10-12 cm × 5-7 cm; weight: ~120-170 g each
  • Right kidney slightly lower than left (due to liver position)
  • Structure: Cortex (outer) → Medulla (inner with pyramids) → Pelvis (funnel-shaped, collects urine)
  • Blood supply: Renal artery (from aorta) → kidney → Renal vein → IVC. Kidneys receive 20-25% of cardiac output!
  • Functional unit: Nephron (~1-1.5 million per kidney)

3. Nephron — Functional Unit

Each nephron consists of Malpighian body (Bowman's capsule + Glomerulus) + Renal tubule (PCT, Loop of Henle, DCT, Collecting duct).

Bowman's Capsule & Glomerulus
  • Glomerulus — tuft of capillaries; blood enters via afferent arteriole (wider) and leaves via efferent arteriole (narrower)
  • The narrower efferent creates high pressure in glomerulus → filtration
  • Filtration membrane: Endothelium + Basement membrane + Podocytes (foot cells) — selectively filters blood → glomerular filtrate (ultrafiltration)
Renal Tubule
  • PCT (Proximal Convoluted Tubule) — Reabsorption of glucose, amino acids, Na⁺, K⁺, HCO₃⁻, Cl⁻, water (65-70%). Also secretes H⁺, NH₃ (acid-base balance)
  • Loop of Henle — Descending limb (water permeable), Ascending limb (impermeable to water, actively transports Na⁺/Cl⁻ out). Creates medullary osmotic gradient for water reabsorption
  • DCT (Distal Convoluted Tubule) — Na⁺ reabsorption (aldosterone-regulated), Ca²⁺ reabsorption (PTH-regulated), H⁺ secretion
  • Collecting Duct — Water reabsorption (ADH-regulated); final concentration of urine

4. Urine Formation

Urine formation involves 3 processes:

  • Glomerular Filtration — Blood filtered in glomerulus → filtrate (~180 L/day) enters Bowman's capsule. Contains all plasma components except proteins & blood cells
  • Reabsorption — Useful substances (glucose, amino acids, water, salts) reabsorbed from tubule back into blood. ~99% of filtrate is reabsorbed!
  • Secretion — Additional wastes (H⁺, K⁺, NH₃, drugs) actively secreted from blood into tubule

Final urine: ~1-1.5 L/day, pH 4.5-8.0, composition: 95% water + 2% urea + 3% salts/others

Key Numbers
Blood filtered per day: 180 L | Urine output: 1-1.5 L | Reabsorption: 99% | Kidneys receive 20-25% of cardiac output | 1-1.5 million nephrons/kidney

5. Countercurrent Mechanism

  • The Loop of Henle and Vasa recta (capillaries around loop) form the countercurrent system
  • Creates a hyperosmotic medullary gradient (300 mOsm in cortex → 1200 mOsm at tip of medulla)
  • This gradient allows concentration of urine — water moves out of collecting duct due to high osmotic pressure in medulla
  • ADH (Vasopressin) increases water permeability of collecting duct → more water reabsorbed → concentrated urine
  • Without ADH → Diabetes Insipidus (dilute urine, excessive thirst)

6. Urinary Bladder & Micturition

  • Ureters — tubes from kidneys to bladder (peristalsis moves urine)
  • Urinary Bladder — muscular sac; capacity ~300-500 mL; stretch receptors trigger urge at ~200-300 mL
  • Urethra — tube from bladder to outside. Males: ~20 cm (also passage for semen), Females: ~4 cm (only urine)
  • Micturition — reflex (spinal + voluntary control). Internal & external urethral sphincters
  • Ureteric openings → bladder; UTI (Urinary Tract Infection) — common, especially in females (shorter urethra)

7. Skin & Lungs as Excretory Organs

Skin
  • Largest organ (~1.5-2 m²); has sweat glands (~2-4 million!)
  • Eccrine sweat glands — all over body; produce hypotonic sweat (water + NaCl + urea)
  • Apocrine sweat glands — armpits, groin; produce thicker secretion (odour after bacterial breakdown)
  • Functions: Thermoregulation (cooling via evaporation), excretion, protection
  • Layers: Epidermis (outer, stratified squamous) → Dermis (inner, connective tissue, blood vessels, glands) → Hypodermis (fat)
Lungs
  • Remove CO₂ and water vapor (as expired air)
  • ~300-500 mL of water lost per day via breathing (insensible water loss)

9. Practice MCQs

1. The functional unit of kidney is:
A) Neuron
B) Nephron
C) Alveolus
D) Villus
Answer: B) Nephron (~1-1.5 million per kidney)
2. Normal urine output per day is:
A) 500 mL
B) 1-1.5 L
C) 2-3 L
D) 180 L
Answer: B) 1-1.5 L/day (180 L is glomerular filtrate)
3. Which hormone controls water reabsorption in kidneys?
A) Aldosterone
B) ADH
C) PTH
D) Thyroxine
Answer: B) ADH (Vasopressin) — increases water permeability of collecting duct
4. Glucose is reabsorbed in:
A) PCT
B) DCT
C) Loop of Henle
D) Collecting duct
Answer: A) PCT (Proximal Convoluted Tubule) — along with amino acids, Na⁺, water
5. Which substance is NOT normally found in urine?
A) Urea
B) Glucose
C) NaCl
D) Water
Answer: B) Glucose — should be completely reabsorbed in PCT (presence in urine = diabetes)
